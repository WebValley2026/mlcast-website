# Community & News

MLCast is built around an open and collaborative community where contributors, researchers, developers, and weather enthusiasts work together to advance machine learning for weather nowcasting. We believe that transparent communication and shared knowledge are just as important as the code itself.

## Stay Connected

The best way to stay informed is by participating in our community. Whether you're a first-time contributor or an experienced researcher, everyone is welcome to join discussions, ask questions, share ideas, and help shape the future of the project.

Our website includes contact information and links to all of our communication channels, making it easy to reach maintainers, join discussions, or get support when you need it.

## Monthly Community Meetings

MLCast hosts **monthly community meetings on Microsoft Teams** where contributors can:

* Hear updates on current development.
* Learn about new models, datasets, and features.
* Discuss ongoing research and future plans.
* Ask questions and share ideas.
* Meet other members of the community.

These meetings are open to anyone interested in the project, regardless of experience level.

## Meeting Notes

To keep everyone informed, meeting notes are published after each monthly meeting. These notes summarize the topics discussed, important decisions, development progress, upcoming milestones, and action items so that contributors who couldn't attend can easily stay up to date.

## Project Updates

As MLCast continues to grow, we'll try to regularly share news about:

* New model releases
* Dataset updates
* Software improvements
* Community milestones
* Research collaborations
* Upcoming events and opportunities

Following these updates is a great way to see what's new and discover where you can contribute.

## Get Involved

Whether you're contributing code, improving documentation, training models, or simply learning about weather AI, there's a place for you in the MLCast community. Visit the contact section of our website to connect with the team, join our discussions, and become part of our growing open-source community.
